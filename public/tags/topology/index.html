Vector Spaces

A vector space $V$ over a field $F$ satisfies these axioms:

$$ \begin{aligned} &\forall \mathbf{u},\mathbf{v} \in V,\ \mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v} \in V \ &\forall \alpha \in F,\ \forall \mathbf{v} \in V,\ \alpha \mathbf{v} \in V \end{aligned} $$

Eigenvalue Problem

For a square matrix $A$, find:

$$ A\mathbf{v} = \lambda\mathbf{v} $$

Where:

  • $\lambda$ is the eigenvalue
  • $\mathbf{v} \neq \mathbf{0}$ is the eigenvector
